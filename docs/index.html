Learn how five major AI paradigms actually work, using Sudoku as the worked example. Everything runs locally in your browser — open the console, read the code, change a number.

Why Sudoku to learn AI?

Sudoku is small enough to fit on one screen, has a single unambiguous correct answer, and is hard for brute force but easy to verify — exactly the properties that make a problem a good teaching example for AI. The same five families of techniques used here (exact search, supervised learning, reinforcement learning, evolutionary computation, and local search) are the same families used in production systems for everything from chip design to drug discovery to game-playing agents.
